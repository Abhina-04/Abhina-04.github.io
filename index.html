Featured Project

SAE BAJA — Roll Cage Design
& Structural Validation

Designing the most safety-critical component of a competition off-road vehicle — where every gram saved must be justified by every simulation run.

As Roll Cage Design Head, I was responsible for ensuring our ATV structure would protect the driver under worst-case load scenarios while staying competitive on weight. Structural validation through ANSYS FEA was the primary engineering tool — and each simulation result was an argument for or against a design decision.

01

Requirements Analysis

Extracted structural constraints from the BAJA SAE rulebook. Defined load cases: frontal impact, rollover, side impact, and torsion.

02

CAD Modelling & Iterations

Parametric roll cage geometry in SolidWorks. Multiple iterations driven by early FEA feedback before finalising geometry.

03

ANSYS Structural Validation

Static structural FEA for each load case. Von Mises stress, deformation, and FoS assessment. Joint geometry iterated to reduce stress concentrations.

04

Compliance & Competition

Final design validated against BAJA SAE standards. Contributed to competition preparation as part of the team.

Reflections

Lessons from Building Things

What engineering projects actually teach you — beyond the tools.

🏎️

Leading Roll Cage Design Taught Me

Every engineering decision is a tradeoff — weight, strength, manufacturability, cost
Leading a workstream means being accountable for decisions made collectively
Simulation accelerates iteration — engineering judgment decides when to stop
Safety-critical design demands a fundamentally different standard of rigor
🤖

Working on AI in Manufacturing Taught Me

Domain expertise is harder to encode than the AI model itself
Industrial AI adoption is constrained by trust, not capability
The engineer's role shifts from doing to validating in AI-augmented workflows
A support portal is a product — it has users, requirements, and an iteration cycle
🌍

Building Simulation Environments Taught Me

A simulation is only as good as how faithfully it models reality
Integration between tools is where most complexity lives
Balancing fidelity vs. performance is itself an engineering problem
Systems thinking means understanding every layer of the tool stack
⚓

Industrial Internships Taught Me

Engineering at scale operates under constraints textbooks don't mention
Maintenance knowledge fundamentally changes how you approach design
Documentation and QA are engineering skills, not administrative ones
Seeing how systems fail in the real world is the best design education
